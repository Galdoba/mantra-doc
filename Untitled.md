# Component Types
Components fall into the following categories: Structural, Utility, Control, Machinery and Drives. Each requires different types of materials in differing
proportions to construct or upgrade.

Structural
Structural components include the hull and internal bulkheads. They require only Structural materials.

Utility
Utility components include staterooms and common areas, and other internal systems such as cargo handling cranes, briefing rooms, small craft grapples
and other systems. Utility components need structural components but only a trivial amount of electronics.

Control
Control systems are electronics-heavy, requiring a considerable amount of technical components in addition to some structural and outfitting supplies.
Control systems include computers, the bridge and also weapons aboard ships that have them.

Machinery
Machinery includes all systems that require considerable amounts of electronics as well as structural components. These include power systems and heavy internal
machinery such as cargo handling cranes.

Drives
Drives include all types of manoeuvre drive and the jump drive. They are expensive items to maintain and upgrade due to their requirement for a great deal of technical materials.
